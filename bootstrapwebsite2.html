It’s no secret that good oral hygiene is important for overall health. After all, the mouth is the gateway to the body. What many people don’t realize, however, is just how important oral hygiene is. Be Gentle With The Strongest!

Achieving and maintaining healthy teeth requires more than just brushing and flossing regularly. A well-rounded approach to oral health also includes paying attention to what you eat and drink. Diet plays a big role in keeping your teeth and gums healthy. Eating a variety of nutrient-rich foods helps your mouth stay clean and free of bacteria that can cause cavities and gum disease. To keep your teeth strong, make sure to include plenty of calcium-rich foods in your diet, such as milk, cheese, yogurt, and leafy green vegetables. You should also consume foods that are high in phosphate, like meat, poultry, eggs, and fish. These minerals help build up tooth enamel. In addition to eating the right foods, it’s also important to limit sugary snacks and drinks. Too much sugar can promote tooth decay by feeding the bacteria in your mouth that create plaque. If you do indulge in sugary treats, be sure to brush your teeth afterwards to remove the sugar from your mouth.

Root canal is a treatment to repair and save a badly damaged or infected tooth instead of removing it. The term "root canal" comes from cleaning of the canals inside a tooth's root. Decades ago, root canal treatments often were painful. With dental advances and local anesthetics, most people have little if any pain during a root canal. In fact, it's probably more painful living with a decayed tooth. Root canal alternatives include extracting the damaged tooth, no further treatment, or replacing the tooth with a dental implant, bridge or removable partial denture.

Teeth have a soft core called dental pulp. The pulp extends from the crown — the visible part of the tooth — to the tip of the tooth's root in the jawbone. The pulp contains nerves, blood vessels and connective tissue. When a tooth is cracked or chipped or has a deep cavity, bacteria can enter the pulp. Injury to the tooth also can cause pulp damage and inflammation. Left untreated, bacteria and decaying material can cause a serious infection or a tooth abscess, leading to pulp death, bone loss and loss of the tooth itself. Signs and symptoms may include swelling around your face and neck, a hole in your tooth, toothache or tooth pain, gum swelling, and temperature sensitivity.

Free Health Checkup

Free oral cancer screening and dental checkup camp were organized by PCER, Chembur. Collaboration with MGM hospitals, Kalamboli, dated 21st March, 2023. The main aim is to raise awareness about oral cancer and promote oral health. These camps typically provide free oral cancer screening, dental checkup etc. During these camps, trained healthcare professionals perform a visual and physical examination of the oral cavity to look for signs of cancer. They referred for further treatment or follow-up care if necessary. They may also provide education and street play on oral health and cancer prevention.

Overall, this camp was a valuable resource for individuals who may not have regular access to oral health care or who may be at risk for oral cancer. These camps help to identify dental issues early on, which can lead to better outcomes and lower costs in the long term.
